TradeGuard

Private multilateral netting + atomic settlement for trade finance — on Canton.

Firms that owe each other can net it down and settle the residual —
without ever showing each other their books.
The problem

Multi-party trade finance is a web of obligations.

Today you either…

  • A
    Settle gross — every obligation moves money. Huge liquidity drag.
  • B
    Net through a trusted central party that sees everyone's positions. A privacy & trust problem.
And cross-leg settlement carries Herstatt risk: you pay, and you don't receive.
The insight · why only on Canton
You can only net positions you're allowed to see. Canton lets an authorized operator net positions the counterparties can't see each other's.

What the agent does

Propose, don't dispose.

  • 1
    Monitors the confidential book it's disclosed to.
  • 2
    Computes the multilateral net → minimal residual transfers.
  • 3
    Proposes a netting batch with an auditable rationale.
  • 4
    Stops — a human approves on-ledger. The agent never moves assets on its own.
  • 5
    Settles atomically — discharge every obligation and move only residual cash, in ONE transaction.

The credibility beat

The ledger checks the agent — not the reverse.

A reasoning agent proposing settlements sounds risky. So the netting contract enforces, on-ledger:

Conservationresidual net per party = the netted obligations. No value invented or lost.
Efficiencyresidual gross ≤ obligation gross. The net can't exceed what it nets.
Fundingan underfunded leg rolls the whole transaction back.
