Cross-currency (FX) co-signed rate

The FX rate is an on-ledger contract signed by every party. A EUR debt nets against a USD debt only at a rate all parties agreed to — the operator can't pick it. Netting conserves value at that rate.

Aggregate caps bound a firm's TOTAL outflow across ALL counterparties — the Basel large-exposure case bilateral limits can't catch. The solver defers the excess; the ledger re-checks at settle.

Privacy — who sees the book live, per party

The operator sees the whole book to compute the net; every firm sees only its own obligations; the ledger itself rejects any under-settlement. No trusted compute, no full-book disclosure — privacy is what makes safe netting possible. Click a row to see refs.
